Mend - Google Design Exercise

A bridge between students and maintenance staff at educational institutions.

Timeframe

1.5 Months | Oct - Dec '19

Role

Designer

Tools

Photoshop | Illustrator | After Effects

Proposal

Presented by Google:
"Your school wants to improve the upkeep of campus facilities by creating a new system for reporting any facilities that may need maintenance or repair. Design an experience that allows students to report building or equipment issues on campus. Consider the process of those filing the report and of those receiving and taking action on the issues."

Solution

After identifying pain points, the main goals were mapped out to be:s

    •   Alleviate inefficient and time-consuming ticket queues
    •   Minimize repetitive tickets to reduce queue overload
    •   Continuously update users and admins on repairs issues while keeping them clear and concise
Based on these specifications, Mend was created to provide two-way communication between students and maintenance staff that would make reporting and repairing issues timely and efficient. With a built in database system, students can receive quick responses as maintenance staff will be able to view their problems immeditely after a ticket is submitted.

Design Process

Low Fidelity

Wire framing using Illustrator.

High Fidelity

Mockups designed using Photoshop to demonstrate final product.

Animations

Animated using After Effects to visualize application.
